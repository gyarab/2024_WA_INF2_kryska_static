League of Legends

League of Legends (LoL) is a popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game developed and published by Riot Games. Released in 2009, it has become one of the most played and well-known esports titles globally.

In League of Legends, two teams, each consisting of five players, compete to destroy the opposing team's Nexus, which is located in their base. The game is set on a map called Summoner’s Rift, and each player controls a unique character known as a champion. These champions have distinct abilities, strengths, and playstyles, which fall into different roles, such as:

  1. Top lane: A solo lane for tanky or bruiser champions.
  2. Mid lane: A solo lane typically for mages or assassins.
  3. Bot lane: A duo lane with an attack-damage carry (ADC) and a support champion.
  4. Jungle: A roaming role that collects buffs and assists other lanes.

During the game, players earn gold and experience by killing minions, champions, and neutral monsters, allowing them to level up and buy items to strengthen their champions. Team coordination, strategy, and skillful play are key to success.

The game also has various modes, including Ranked, ARAM (All Random All Mid), and rotating game modes, alongside frequent updates, new champions, and in-game events to keep gameplay fresh. League of Legends is free-to-play with optional purchases for cosmetic items, such as skins for champions.

Its competitive scene, LoL Esports, is massive, with tournaments like the League of Legends World Championship (Worlds) attracting millions of viewers globally.
